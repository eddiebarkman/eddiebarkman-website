A Satirical Comedy

about the

Virgin Mary
A feisty, career-driven Mary of Nazareth resists the calling from God to bear his son in order to pursue her dream of fashion greatness.

Synopsis

In this age-old tale with a twist, Mary of Nazareth is strong-willed, free-spirited with dreams of her own. Her plans to get out of the hood and pursue a fashion career in Egypt, where women have equal rights, are derailed when a narcissistic Angel Gabriel tells her she will conceive a child through the Holy Spirit.


Distraught by the awful news she heads home, only to find out she’s being married off to Joseph The Carpenter in exchange for a donkey. Her journey into motherhood becomes real when she hitches a ride to visit her Aunt Elizabeth, who is in her 70’s and also miraculously pregnant.


A Mel Brooks meets Key and Peel farcical depiction of the events surrounding the immaculate conception and the birth of Jesus from Mary’s point of view greatness.

On her subsequent trip to Bethlehem, she encounters colorful characters, including the clueless, pot-smoking King Herod, who’s determined to get rid of the Messiah; the three nutty, not-so-wise men chasing after a star in hopes of meeting baby Jesus, and a love-starved Shepherd, who’s good intentions are misread by Mary and Joseph.
