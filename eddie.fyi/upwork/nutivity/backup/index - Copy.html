about the

A Mel Brooks meets Key and Peel farcical depiction of the events surrounding the immaculate conception and the birth of Jesus from Mary’s point of view greatness.

Synopsis

In this age-old tale with a twist, Mary of Nazareth is strong-willed, free-spirited with dreams of her own. Her plans to get out of the hood and pursue a fashion career in Egypt, where women have equal rights, are derailed when a narcissistic Angel Gabriel tells her she will conceive a child through the Holy Spirit.

Distraught by the awful news she heads home, only to find out she’s being married off to Joseph The Carpenter in exchange for a donkey. Her journey into motherhood becomes real when she hitches a ride to visit her Aunt Elizabeth, who is in her 70’s and also miraculously pregnant.

On her subsequent trip to Bethlehem, she encounters colorful characters, including the clueless, pot-smoking King Herod, who’s determined to get rid of the Messiah; the three nutty, not-so-wise men chasing after a star in hopes of meeting baby Jesus, and a love-starved Shepherd, who’s good intentions are misread by Mary and Joseph.

Born in Mumbai, Prashant is a Miami-based writer. Most recently, he won the "Best Screenplay Pitch" award in 2017 and 2018 at the Miami Media and Film Market- in 2017 for his comedy screenplay Nut-ivity, currently in early stages of production. He is also one of the screenwriters who collaborated in the full-length feature film Horror Time, produced by James Franco. In 2010, he co-produced the award-winning film I Am, starring Juhi Chawla, Manisha Koirala, Nandita Das and Rahul Bose. Prashant studied creative writing at the University of Miami and screenwriting at Studio4 in Los Angeles and New York. He is a proud feminist, dog-lover, and a citizen of the world.

Maddie Shapiro is a New York based film producer and writer. Her film Catfight, starring Sandra Oh, Anne Heche, and Alicia Silverstone, premiered at the 2016 Toronto International Film Festival and opened theatrically March 2017. Her latest project Black Magic For White Boys premiered at the 2017 Tribeca Film Festival. In 2013 she won the Arthur Krim Memorial Award for Outstanding Producer. She has worked with top producers, including Lydia Dean Pilcher on the Disney film Queen of Katwe starring David Oyelowo and Lupita Nyong’o, and Jonathan Hock on Season 1 of the Emmy-nominated series MLS INSIDER.
